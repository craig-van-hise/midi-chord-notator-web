# Product Requirements Prompt (PRP) — ✅ Completed

## 1. Project Context & Objectives
The previous implementation of the Note Selection Engine successfully implemented the rotational and diatonic math, but hit-testing failed critically. Due to SMuFL font metric baseline offsets, the DOM bounding boxes of the notes do not align with their visual glyphs, causing a massive Y-coordinate offset (clicking the bass clef selects treble clef notes). The objective is to decouple hit-testing from the visual text nodes by introducing mathematically placed "Phantom Hitboxes."

## 2. Technical Decisions & Dependencies
* **Phantom Hitbox Architecture:** You are strictly forbidden from attaching `onClick`, `onPointerDown`, or `data-note-id` attributes directly to the SMuFL text elements. The text elements must be styled with `pointer-events: none;`.
* **Hitbox Sizing:** For every rendered note, you will render a secondary, absolute-positioned `<div>`. This is the Phantom Hitbox. It will be completely transparent (`opacity-0` or `bg-transparent`), sized to `width: calc(var(--staff-space) * 1.5)` and `height: calc(var(--staff-space) * 1.2)`.
* **Alignment:** The Phantom Hitbox must use the exact same `top` and `left` CSS mathematical positioning as its visual counterpart, but utilize `transform: translate(-50%, -50%)` to ensure its geometric center is perfectly pinned to the mathematical pitch line.
* **Event Delegation:** All `data-note-id`, `data-pitch`, and pointer events will be delegated exclusively to these Phantom Hitboxes.
